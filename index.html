About Us

Our Story

Carleton Natyam was founded in 2018 by a group of passionate students dedicated to celebrating and sharing the rich tradition of Bharatanatyam. Our club brings together dancers of all levels, from beginners to advanced practitioners, united by our love for this classical Indian dance form.

We strive to create a supportive community where members can learn, practice, and perform together while honoring the cultural heritage and artistic depth of Bharatanatyam. Through workshops, performances, and cultural events, we aim to promote awareness and appreciation of this beautiful art form on campus and beyond.

What is Bharatanatyam?

The Classical Dance of South India

Bharatanatyam is one of the oldest classical dance forms originating from Tamil Nadu in South India. With a history spanning over 2,000 years, it combines precise footwork (nritta), expressive storytelling (abhinaya), and symbolic hand gestures (mudras) to create a visually stunning and emotionally rich performance art.

The dance form is characterized by its fixed upper torso, bent legs, and elaborate footwork. Dancers tell stories through a complex language of gestures and expressions, often depicting mythological themes and spiritual concepts from Hindu texts.

Bharatanatyam performances are traditionally accompanied by Carnatic music, with rhythmic patterns set by the mridangam (drum) and melodic framework provided by vocals and instruments like the veena and flute.
